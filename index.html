Building the Space We Needed

When I looked around, I realized something was missing. Nigerian women needed a space. A real space where we could speak our truth without judgment, without fear, without having to shrink ourselves to fit into boxes that were never made for us.

That realization sparked the vision for Spill. I saw brilliant women holding back their thoughts, their experiences, their questions because where do you go to talk about the things that matter? Where do you share your dating stories without being shamed? Where do you ask about your body without being judged? Where do you navigate workplace challenges with people who truly get it?

Spill is that space. It's our space.

This is a platform for the girls' girls, where femininity doesn't just exist, it THRIVES. Here, you can talk about everything: dating adventures, those green and red flags we all need to discuss, your body and health, life experiences that shaped you, education, work challenges, and yes, even the men who either lift us up or let us down.

The best part? It's completely anonymous. Your data stays secret, everything is end to end encrypted. No judgment, no exposure, just pure, authentic connection with women who understand your journey.

I've been quietly working on this project, pouring my heart into bringing you something special. Something that honors who we are and gives us the freedom to be exactly that, unapologetically.

We're launching soon in Nigeria, and I cannot wait to watch our community flourish.

Get ready to spill, sisters. Your safe space is coming.

With love and anticipation,
Oluwanifemi Ojo
Founder, Spill
