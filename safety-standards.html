Child Safety Standards

Last updated: September 9, 2025

Spill is a women-only, anonymous community. We maintain a zero-tolerance policy for Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) and any sexual exploitation or endangerment of minors. This page outlines our prevention practices, reporting channels, moderation process, and legal compliance commitments.

1) Scope & Definitions

  • Minor/Child: Any person under the age of 18.
  • CSAM: Any content (text, image, video, audio, link, or AI-generated media) that depicts or sexually exploits a minor.
  • Grooming: Any behavior intended to gain a minor’s trust for sexual exploitation.

2) Zero-Tolerance Policy (Prohibited Content)

  • Any CSAM or sexualized content involving minors, including AI-generated or “synthetic” media.
  • Sexual solicitation of, or by, anyone under 18; requests for sexual images; sharing sexual stories involving identifiable minors.
  • Grooming, sextortion, trafficking, or doxxing a minor; instructions to evade parental/guardian protections.
  • Links to sites, storage, or platforms hosting CSAM.

Violations result in immediate content removal and account termination. Where required, we report to relevant authorities.

3) Age Requirements

  • Spill is intended for adult women. Users must be 18+.
  • Accounts suspected of being underage are suspended pending verification and permanently removed if under 18.

4) How to Report Child Safety Concerns

In-app: Use the Report/Flag option on any post, reply, or profile. Choose “Child Safety” as the reason.
Email: oluwafunbi.onaeko@gmail.com (subject line: “Child Safety Report”).

Include relevant usernames, post links, timestamps, and any context. Do not download or redistribute suspected CSAM.

5) Our Moderation Workflow & Timelines

  • Immediate actions: We promptly hide or block access to reported content and restrict involved accounts.
  • Review: Trained moderators review child-safety reports with priority.
  • Outcomes: Removal, account termination, device/account bans, and report to authorities when applicable.
  • Appeals: Users may appeal non-CSAM child-safety takedowns via email. CSAM decisions are not reversible.

6) Prevention & Detection Measures

  • Access controls: Women-only verification; under-18 not permitted.
  • Proactive measures: Automated signals and manual review to detect grooming, sextortion, and child endangerment patterns.
  • Link safety: We restrict or remove links to known abuse domains and suspicious file hosts.
  • Repeat abusers: Permanent bans; attempts to rejoin are blocked where feasible.

7) Cooperation with Authorities

We comply with applicable laws and reporting obligations. When legally required or appropriate, we report CSAM and imminent threats to relevant national authorities (for example, the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children in the U.S.) and work with law enforcement as permitted by law.

8) Evidence Preservation & Privacy

  • We preserve minimal information necessary to comply with legal obligations and to assist lawful investigations.
  • We do not redistribute or circulate suspected CSAM; we securely restrict internal access.
  • User data related to reports is handled under strict need-to-know access controls and encrypted in transit.

9) Education & Resources

  • Never share images of minors in compromising contexts. Report suspicious requests immediately.
  • If someone may be in immediate danger, contact your local emergency number first, then report to us.

11) Legal Compliance Statement

By using Spill, you agree to our Terms and Community Guidelines. Spill complies with applicable child protection and reporting laws in the regions where we operate and with Google Play’s child safety standards. We review and update these standards as laws and best practices evolve.

If you are a regulator or law enforcement agency with a lawful request, contact us at the email above and include your official details and the legal authority for your request.
